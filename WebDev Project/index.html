Overview of the Caribbean

The Caribbean is a region comprising islands and coastal territories in the Americas, predominantly influenced by its relationship with the Caribbean Sea. It played a pivotal role in the early interactions between Europeans and indigenous peoples of the New World, initiated by Columbus' arrival, which led to subsequent exploration and contact.

From the late 15th to early 16th centuries, Europeans exploited the region for mineral and agricultural resources, catalyzing significant demographic shifts as indigenous populations were supplanted by Africans. Defining the Caribbean presents challenges due to its diverse islands, places, and peoples, each following unique historical trajectories.

One notable pattern is the demographic collapse due to the introduction of European and African diseases, causing indigenous populations to plummet by as much as 90%. This led to the importation of African laborers via the transatlantic slave trade to meet the demand for labor.
